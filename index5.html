Where Life, Death, and Sin Intersect

The books I gravitate toward are heavy with tension and moral decay. Stories like Roommate and Kasak Angkarn uncover how ordinary lives can slip into crime, betrayal, and irreversible violence. Trust becomes a weakness, and survival often demands silence or cruelty.

In A Time to Kill, justice blurs into vengeance, forcing the reader to confront whether any action can truly be righteous. Tears of Life lingers on the final breaths of existence, while The Midnight Library offers not hope, but the quiet torment of endless regret. Even Dark Fairy Tales strip innocence away, revealing fear and desire beneath familiar myths. These stories do not offer salvation — only reflection.
